Grabby

An E-Commerce solution. This app enables users shop online for a better experience.

This is a Saas project designed to enable users find products and services advertised for purchase. It enables users schedule delivery at convenience, track order In-app once purchase has been made.

Visual Design

0.1 Objective

This project will ensure users make daily online purchase without hassle at the comfort of their homes.
Grabby focuses on easing frustrations these shoppers experience by categorising products which enables easy search, scheduling a delivery at convenience and also tracking a product once order has been shipped out.

0.2 The Problem

In Nigeria , Shopping online has been a norm in recent times. People choose to shop any product of choice from the comfort of their homes which has made stores less crowded and also made life easy for shoppers and business owners. However, shopping online can be difficult most times especially when shoppers get limited service as such leaves them frustrated and unsatisfied.

55M in Nigeria shop online
80% cannot schedule delivery at convenience
60% unable to track order
How might a SAAS help shoppers relieve and recover from shopping experience?
How might the ability to track an order encourage users to shop more?
How might being able to schedule delivery at convenience make customers return?

0.3 The Goal

The Grabby project tends to address the above problem statement by properly categorising these products and also offering logistics services to the comfort of shoppers locations for easier and better experience, thereby leaving customers/shoppers satisfied after a shopping experience.

0.4 User Research

22 interviews
15 interviews valid survey responses
7 one-on-one interviews

It is important that users are satisfied after shopping a product/service. From interviews carried out, users are not completely satisfied after shopping due to:

unavailability when product arrives due to schedule conflict
Inability to track product when shipped out,
Inability to contact delivery agent.

User persona development

0.8 Design Guideline

DM Sans is the only font used through the design. For the sake of development sanity, base REM value is 16px which is default for major browsers. DM Sans is selected because of its simplicity and readability. Pink and Blue are selected as the primary colors based due to contrast compatibity which defeats any of the other complimentary color combination. Blue and Pink also conveys the friendly and informative nature Grabby can bring.

0.9 Outcome

The brief was to design a unique and simple website that solves the problem of delay, schedule conflict and frustrations. By following the UX process (researching ideas, sketching, choosing design guidelines, information architecture, creating prototypes and user testing), the idea for a simple and user-friendly was implemented and the goal was achieved.

10. Prototype

Onboarding and Authentication

  • Users will fill in their name, email address, phone number and choose password. They will arrive at the homepage when registration is complete.
  • New users will be required to enable location for easy product search. They will have the option to turn location on or off.

Homepage

  • By arriving at the homepage, users will see products displayed by merchants and shows brief details about each item.
  • Users also have a search option for easier product or merchant searches based on Geo-location or broader searches enabled during registration.
  • Users can also access profile, favourites and livechat service using the navigation bar.

Ordering process

  • Users are able to view more products options and scan through other merchants products.
  • They will choose a prefered category and select their preference accordingly by adding to cart.

Reviews

  • Users can read reviews to see feedback on products before or after purchase.
  • Users are also able to add a review.

Schedule Order & Checkout

  • After selecting a product of choice, users can proceed to order confirmation from merchant by scheduling same day delivery (Home or pick-up) or delivery at convenience where they select date and time of choice.
  • Users can also drop a direct note to the dispatch agent for better address clarification or further details.

Order Tracking

  • Users can track their order delivery in real time and make direct contact with the delivery agent once order has been confirmed.

Profile

  • Users are able to view cart, order history, see notifications, view favourite items saved, see available coupons, make a review and read through reviews.
  • They are also able to edit profle and make possible changes when and where required.

Password

  • Users can reset password in-app via settings or in a case of ‘’forgot password’’
  • An OTP is sent to the users email address and they can login with new details.

11.Iterations

We recruited 5 participants from the slack community to conduct remote moderated usability testing on the designs. During the test, we asked them to complete a series of tasks that helped us to understand their thought process and see if they could accoumplish the goal of the project.. This was also the perfect time to ask about how they feel about the prototype.

After usability testing, we discovered that having the form field in the top part of the page instead of the social sign up options, helped the users to access the social sign up options in the solution with ease. The options are retained of course, but as a secondary option.

After usability testing, we discovered the need to provide the dispatch rider’s contact information. This will enable easier location of package drop off point and collection of package upon the rider’s arrival.

12.Takeaways
